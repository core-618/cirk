# Why Story Points Break with AI

Story points were built to estimate human effort.

That made sense when software delivery was constrained by:

- manual coding time
- human throughput
- team velocity

In AI-assisted development, those assumptions are weaker.

---

## What changed

AI compresses implementation time.
A task that once took days to draft may now take minutes to produce.

But speed does not remove execution risk.
It changes where the risk lives.

What matters now is not just effort. It is:

- how much context the agent must absorb
- how many iterations are needed to reach a correct result
- how much human review is required
- how risky the integration or rollout is

---

## Why story points become misleading

Two tasks may both be called a “3” in a traditional planning system, while being completely different in AI execution terms.

Example:

- small UI text fix
- auth flow rewrite touching multiple systems

They may look similar in effort if AI drafts both quickly.
But they are radically different in review and deployment risk.

---

## What CIRK does instead

CIRK models the execution reality directly:

- **C** — Context
- **I** — Iteration
- **R** — Review
- **K** — Integration Risk

This makes autonomy and governance explicit.

---

## The shift

Story points answer:

> How much human effort is this?

CIRK answers:

> How should this be executed safely?

That is the more useful question in AI-native development.
